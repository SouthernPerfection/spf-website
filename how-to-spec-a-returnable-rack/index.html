How to spec a returnable rack.

Everything to nail down before you request a quote — so you get the right rack the first time. Pin down the part, the load plan, and the freight loop up front, and you get a rack that protects the part, ships dense, and comes back trip after trip.

Get the guide + checklist →

The five things

What to nail down.

1 · The part

Overall size (L×W×H), the heaviest part's weight, how it sits, and any fragile or finish-critical surfaces that can't touch steel.

2 · The load plan

Parts per rack and per layer, separators or dunnage, whether racks stack, nest, or fold, and the empty-return footprint.

3 · Logistics & freight loop

Annual volume or trips, ship-from/ship-to lanes, and the truck, trailer, or container the rack must fit. Design to the trailer.

4 · Handling & environment

Forklift, pallet jack, or crane; load/unload height for ergonomics; indoor/outdoor; wash, heat, or corrosive exposure — that drives material & finish.

5 · The returnable payback

Expected trips and service life, and cost per trip versus expendable packaging plus the transit damage it lets through. Count damage and scrap, not just packaging.

Copy this, fill it in, send it

Your rack spec checklist.

Have most of these and we can start a concept — you don't need every line. Get the free PDF guide and one-page checklist:

  • Part(s) the rack holds — print, CAD, or a clear photo
  • Part size & weight — especially the heaviest part
  • Parts per rack / per shipment
  • Annual volume or number of trips
  • Ship-from & ship-to lanes — and the truck / trailer / container
  • Returnable or one-way? — stack / nest / fold
  • Handling & environment — forklift vs. pallet jack; indoor / outdoor
  • Current pain — transit damage, ergonomics, a supplier falling short
  • Timing — a current need or an upcoming launch date
